My Projects

ALERT SYSTEMS FOR WOMEN IN VEHICLES

This project focuses on enhancing women's safety in public and private transportation by developing a smart, real-time alert system. The system is designed to detect distress situations and automatically trigger alerts to emergency contacts or authorities through GPS-based location tracking and GSM communication. Additional safety features include panic button integration, vibration/motion sensing, and voice-triggered alerts. The goal is to provide a reliable and proactive safety mechanism for women commuters, especially in isolated or dangerous situations.

PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS OF AODV,DSR, AND DSDV ROUTING PROTOCOLS IN MANET USING NS3 & MATLAB

As part of my academic project, I conducted a comprehensive performance evaluation of three widely-used routing protocols in Mobile Ad-hoc Networks (MANET): AODV, DSR, and DSDV. Using NS-3 for simulation and MATLAB for data analysis and visualization, I analyzed key performance metrics including Throughput, Packet Delivery Ratio (PDR), End-to-End Delay, and Routing Overhead. This project enhanced my understanding of wireless network dynamics and protocol behavior under varying mobility and traffic conditions.
